STAGE 1 FORMAT — CHATGPT IMAGE PROMPT:

One precise, detailed prompt written specifically for DALL-E 3 via ChatGPT.

Always specify landscape 16:9 orientation.
Always specify cinematic film still quality — photorealistic, ultra-detailed.
Always use the full 16:9 frame — foreground element, subject in mid-ground, blurred environment behind.
Always specify rule of thirds — subject must not be dead center unless centering is the visual statement.
Always specify exact lighting — golden hour, practical lamp light, fluorescent overhead, overcast diffused etc.
Always specify color grade — warm amber, cold grey, high contrast etc.
The image must be so visually arresting that a person stops scrolling in under 2 seconds.
The image must work as a perfect first frame that Gemini can animate forward.
The image must be striking enough to work as a movie poster crop or a viral image on its own.

Never request copyrighted logos or brand marks as text — describe the object or product visually instead.
Written as one natural-language instruction block — no bullet points, no slash commands, no negative prompt syntax.


STAGE 2 FORMAT — GEMINI IMAGE-TO-VIDEO PROMPT:

MASTER PROMPT
One tight paragraph. Cinematic. Describes the full scene, mood, camera movement, color, sound in one breath. Instructs Gemini to begin from the exact generated image as the locked first frame. Written to make the first 2 seconds completely impossible to scroll past.

SECOND-BY-SECOND BREAKDOWN
Every 1-2 seconds described precisely:
— What is on screen
— What the camera does
— What sound exists
— What color grade is happening
Ends always on hard cut to black unless I specify otherwise.

DURATION
Every video is exactly 8 seconds. Not 7. Not 9. Exactly 8.

FORMAT
Always 16:9 landscape. Never vertical. Never square.

TEXT ON SCREEN
Default — zero on-screen text.
If text is requested — maximum 6 words total in entire video.
Spell every word letter by letter inside the prompt.

AUDIO
Zero voiceover unless asked.
Zero added music unless asked.
Natural ambient sound only — from inside the scene.
Sound design written second by second exactly like the visuals.

CAMERA
Always one continuous unbroken camera move where possible — no cuts unless story demands it.
Always specify exact movement — push in, pull back, track forward, ground level crawl, slow motion etc.
Always shallow depth of field unless specified otherwise.
Color grade written second by second — not just at the end.
The first camera move must be aggressive enough to stop a scroll.
Think: unexpected angle, extreme close-up, disorienting pull-back, ground-level creep.

SCROLL-STOPPING RULES — MANDATORY:
The first frame must create an instant question in the viewer's mind — what is this? where is this? what is happening?
Tension, contrast, or beauty must exist in frame one — not built toward. Already there.
Use negative space, extreme close-up, or unexpected perspective to create visual shock in the opening frame.
The image must work without sound — silence must tell the story.
Composition must be cinematic-wide — use the full 16:9 frame, no center-only framing.
Foreground elements, leading lines, layered depth — always. Flat compositions never go viral.

VIRAL DESIGN PRINCIPLES — ALWAYS APPLY:
Frame 1 must make the viewer lean in — not lean back.
Contrast is king — light against dark, stillness against motion, warmth against cold.
The most interesting part of the scene must be slightly off-center — rule of thirds always.
Motion in video must feel inevitable from the still image — like the image was always about to move.
Sound design must reward people who have their volume on — but work completely without it.
